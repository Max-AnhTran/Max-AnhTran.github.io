Top 5 places to visit in Tallinn

  1. Old Town (Vanalinn): Wander through Tallinn's charming Old Town, a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its medieval architecture, cobblestone streets, and historic landmarks.
  2. Toompea Castle (Toompea Loss): Explore the historic Toompea Castle, overlooking the city from its hilltop perch and housing the Estonian Parliament and various government institutions.
  3. Alexander Nevsky Cathedral: Admire the stunning Orthodox architecture of the Alexander Nevsky Cathedral, an iconic symbol of Tallinn with its distinct onion domes and intricate fa√ßade.
  4. Kadriorg Palace and Park: Stroll through the scenic Kadriorg Park and visit the elegant Kadriorg Palace, built by Peter the Great for his wife Catherine I, now home to the Estonian Art Museum's foreign collection.
  5. Tallinn City Walls and Towers: Discover Tallinn's medieval fortifications, including the well-preserved city walls and defensive towers, offering panoramic views of the city and its surroundings.

Transport

In Tallinn, public transportation is convenient and accessible. The Tallinn public transport system includes buses, trams, and trolleybuses, providing efficient service throughout the city.

ESTONIA

Estonia, a Baltic gem, captivates with its blend of medieval charm and modern innovation. From the medieval streets of Tallinn to the pristine beauty of its forests and coastline, Estonia offers a diverse array of experiences for travelers. Discover the rich cultural heritage of this enchanting land, explore its vibrant arts scene, and savor the flavors of Estonian cuisine. Whether you're wandering through historic towns or exploring the unspoiled wilderness, Estonia promises unforgettable adventures at every turn.
